The FixMyPaths App - Testers Wanted!

The FixMyPaths Android app allows you to report the problems on Hampshire rights of way while out on a walk. Say you come across a missing signpost, or a blocked path, you can fire up the app and report the problem directly to Hampshire County Council (it is the intention to expand out of Hampshire in the future). The app will contact the FixMyPaths server to find out if there is a nearby right of way, and if there is, you will be given the chance to enter the problem report. The report will be stored in FixMyPaths' own database and emailed to Hampshire County Council. The app is in an early stage of development and has been tested by myself live in-the-field, but I am looking for additional testers. So if you live in or near Hampshire, like walking, and have an Android 2.1+ phone, please download the app and get testing!

Instructions to use the app

Disclaimer: Installing the FixMyPaths app to your phone is at your own risk. It is software at an early stage of development; it appears to be stable in tests but please do not blame me if it blows up your phone. With that warning over, here are the instructions on how to install and use the app.

  1. First of all configure your phone to allow installation of non-Market (Google Play) apps. This will be under Settings: on a Samsung Galaxy SIII, it is under "Security and Lock Screen". On the HTC Hero, it's under "Applications". So try a settings category that looks like either of those two.
  2. If you have access to wifi, it's easy. Simply run the app within a wifi area and when prompted, download the map data file. When in use, FixMyPaths stores its maps locally on the phone rather than downloading them from a web server, but obviously you have to download the map onto the phone initally! Do the same with the style file (file which defines the look and feel of the map).
  3. If you do not have access to wifi, it's a little trickier as the map file is rather large (around 9 megabytes) so you need to download the map file from a desktop or laptop and copy it to the phone's SD card. Connect your phone up to your desktop or laptop and find the SD card folder (on the Samsung Galaxy SIII you should select the Phone folder instead, as on this model the map is stored on the actual phone). Inside this folder, make a folder called "openhants". Download the map file from here and place it in the "openhants" folder. If you are having difficulty with this step please email me on freemapinfo at gmail dot com.
  4. You should now be ready to go. Ensure that GPS is switched on, fill in your name and email in the preferences (menu-preferences), find somewhere suitable and report your problem! (for the testing period, this may be a "fake" problem). There will soon be a "Download existing problems" option which ensures that you do not try to report an existing problem by showing them on the map.
  5. Please report your experiences to me, Nick Whitelegg at freemapinfo at gmail dot com.

System requirements

Android 2.1+ needed. I have tested it on two devices: on the one hand an HTC Hero and on the other a Samsung Galaxy SIII. On the Hero the map drawing is rather slow and prone to occasional crashes while on the SIII it runs smoothly. I'd be interested to know what performance is like on different "in-between" models, again please email me (freemapinfo at gmail dot com).
