OpenTrailView :: About

What is OpenTrailView?

OpenTrailView is a project to collect Android Photo Spheres and combine them with OpenStreetMap data to produce virtual tours of the countryside, in which users can navigate from one Photo Sphere to the next. The idea is basically to produce something similar to Google Street View, but for footpaths rather than roads, and (to avoid creating silly amounts of data) showing the Photo Spheres at key points only, such as junctions, views or other points of interest, rather than every few metres.

How to contribute

Contributing is easy if you have a recent Android device. Simply:

  • Sign up for an account
  • Make sure the Photo Sphere camera app is installed on your device. For some Google Nexus devices, it's probably there already. If you have another modern Android device such as the Galaxy SIII or S4, you'll need to install the Photo Sphere camera app separately, as the default camera app does not take Photo Spheres. This has been made available by a third party, see here.
  • Take your Photo Sphere at your desired location. Ensure that your GPS is on, because at present Photo Spheres must be tagged with latitude and longitude to be accepted by OpenTrailView.
  • Upload your Photo Sphere from the main page, after logging in. Note that all Photo Spheres need to be authorised (to guard against questionable content being uploaded). Once authorised your Photo Sphere will be able to be viewed; it will be shown by a camera icon on the map.

Supported browsers

Current versions of Firefox and Chrome are supported. Sorry, for the moment Internet Explorer isn't supported - it will at some point in the future.

Current status and Future plans

Right now, OpenTrailView shows uploaded Photo Spheres on the map . You can view a Photo Sphere by clicking on the camera icon. You can also show the nearest photo sphere to a given location by clicking the "pano view" link, though given there are only a small number of spheres loaded right now, this might be some considerable distance away!

The functionality to link Photo Spheres using the underlying OSM data is not yet implemented, but is intended to be soon. Note that on this server, this linking together will only work for certain areas of the UK, and not globally, as, due to server limitations, only OSM data for certain parts of the UK is included; see here. Because of this, you might be interested in setting up an OpenTrailView server for your own country; see here.
