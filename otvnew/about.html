OpenTrailView2 :: About

What is OpenTrailView?

OpenTrailView is a project to collect Android Photo Spheres and link them (using OpenStreetMap mapping as a guide) to produce virtual tours of the countryside, in which users can navigate from one Photo Sphere to the next. The idea is basically to produce something similar to Google Street View, but for footpaths rather than roads, and (to avoid creating silly amounts of data which servers cannot handle) showing the Photo Spheres at key points only, such as junctions, attractive views or other points of interest, rather than every few metres.

The original OpenTrailView appeared in 2010 and was presented at SOTM Girona that year; however it required a long and cumbersome process to contribute panoramas. Users had to use regular cameras, take panoramic shots and stitch them together using third-party stitching software, as well as taking a GPS trace to place the panoramas by comparing timestamps. With Photo Spheres doing most of that work for you, the contribution process should hopefully be a lot more straightforward!

How to contribute

Contributing is easy if you have a reasonably recent (probably 4.x) Android device. Simply:

  • Sign up for an account
  • Make sure the Photo Sphere camera app is installed on your device. For recent Google Nexus devices, it should be there already. If you have another modern Android device such as the Galaxy SIII or S4, you'll need to install the Photo Sphere camera app separately, as the default camera app does not take Photo Spheres. This has been made available by a third party, see here.
  • Take your Photo Sphere at your desired location. Ensure that your GPS is on, because at present Photo Spheres must be tagged with latitude and longitude to be accepted by OpenTrailView.
  • Upload your Photo Sphere from the main page, after logging in. Note that all Photo Spheres need to be authorised (to guard against questionable content being uploaded). Once authorised your Photo Sphere will be able to be viewed; it will be shown by a camera icon on the map.

Supported Browsers and Issues

This software is in an early stage and therefore there are currently a few issues:

  • Current versions of Firefox and Chrome are supported, as well as Internet Explorer 10. Sorry, for the moment older versions of Internet Explorer are not supported.
  • Loading Photo Spheres is currently rather slow. Hopefully a solution can be found to this by tweaking the underlying Photo Sphere library.
  • There are sometimes some odd glitches where map tiles don't load properly for a while once you have viewed a Photo Sphere. This seems to happen on both Linux and Windows and all of Firefox, Chrome and IE. I'm guessing it might be a memory issue and again something that could probably be fixed by tweaking the underlying library.

Current status and Future plans

Right now, OpenTrailView shows uploaded Photo Spheres on the map . You can view a Photo Sphere by clicking on the camera icon. You can also show the nearest photo sphere to a given location by clicking the "pano view" link, though given there are only a small number of spheres loaded right now, this might be some considerable distance away!

The functionality to link Photo Spheres is not yet implemented, but is intended to be soon. This will involve users linking spheres on the map interface by connecting them with lines.

IMPORTANT - linking with OSM is key!

The idea of linking the Photo Spheres to provide virtual tours of off-road areas is the whole point of the site. Please only upload a Photo Sphere if it was taken on a recognised off-road route of some kind, such as:

  • a countryside trail or track;
  • a recognised climbing or trekking route (e.g. up a mountain, or through wild country);
  • or an urban path such as in a city park.
If you are interested in a general Photo Sphere sharing site, please visit SphereShare.net.

However, there does not have to be an existing OSM way there already. It just has to be a route that would make sense as an OSM way. So if it's unmapped, why not survey it for OSM too!
